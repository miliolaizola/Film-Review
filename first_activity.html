First Activity

Match the types of films with their definitions.

  1. Documentary
  2. Western
  3. Action
  4. Science Fiction
  5. Adventure
  6. Musical
  7. Comedy

  1. In these films the heroes are pushed into a series of events that typically include violence, extended fighting, physical feats, and frantic chases.
  2. These films are like action films, but they take place in exotic places. They could be about pirates, historical adventures such as sailing around the world and space exploration.
  3. The focus is on humor. These films are made to make the audience laugh through amusement.
  4. This is a non-fictional film that investigates some real-life story. The objective is to instruct, educate, or maintain a historical record.
  5. These films use speculative, fictional science-based depictions. The most popular ones are about extraterrestrial lifeforms, alien worlds, time travel, robots or other technologies.
  6. These films are set mainly in the latter half of the 19th century in the Western United States. Its stories commonly center on the life of a cowboy or gunfighter armed with a revolver and a rifle who rides a horse.
  7. Music and songs by the characters are interwoven into the narrative, sometimes accompanied by dancing (e.g: Singin’ in the Rain, 1954).
